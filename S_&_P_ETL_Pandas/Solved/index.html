    Summary: S&P 500 in 2020
    - JB. Stox & Watson 09/01/2020

    The bulls of Wall Street have rejected this years' dismal economic reality, fastening their seatbelts to the 'Roaring Twenties' Part II. The economy suffered its biggest contraction on record in the second quarter, and corporate profits have plunged. The unemployment rate hovered just above 10% last month, retail spending fell off and manufacturing data decreased.

    Some of those indicators have begun to improve, supporting the argument that the economy’s recovery will be V-shaped. Earnings, for one, are expected to contract at more modest levels for the remainder of the year before beginning to climb in the first quarter of 2021. Unemployment levels and sales figures have all bounced off their lows in June and July. Still, the S&P 500’s stride back to its high has left the stock market priced to a T and severely exposed to another beating, some analysts say.
